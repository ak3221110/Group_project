Historical Sites

Golghar

Location: Patna, Bihar, India

Built: 1786 by Captain John Garstin

Significance: A massive granary built to prevent famine in the region.

Architecture: A stupa-shaped structure with a height of 29 meters and a spiral staircase of 145 steps leading to the top.

Historical Background: Constructed during British rule, it was intended to be the first of many granaries, but no others were built.

Unique Features: Offers a panoramic view of Patna and the Ganges River from the top.

Ashoka Pillar

Location: Found in various places across India, including Vaishali, Sarnath, and Delhi.

Significance: Built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE to spread his message of Dhamma (righteousness).

Architecture: Made of polished sandstone, often featuring inscriptions and the famous Lion Capital, which is now India's national emblem.

Historical Background: These pillars were erected at Buddhist monasteries and important sites related to Buddha’s life.

Unique Features: Some pillars contain Ashoka’s edicts, promoting moral values and governance principles.

Munger Fort

Location: Munger, Bihar, India

Significance: A historic fort built on a rocky hillock along the Ganges River.

Architecture: Features massive stone walls, gates, and ancient structures, including religious monuments.

Historical Background: Believed to have been built during the early rule of the Slave dynasty, later controlled by various rulers including the Tughlaqs, Mughals, and British.

Unique Features: Houses the Tomb of Pir Shah Nufa, Palace of Shah Suja, and an ancient temple called Chandisthana.

Darbhanga Fort

Location: Darbhanga, Bihar, India

Significance: A historic fort that was once the residence of the Darbhanga Raj family.

Architecture: Built using red bricks, the fort has walls stretching 1 km long and 500 meters wide.

Historical Background: Constructed in the early 20th century by Maharaja Sir Kameshwar Singh of Darbhanga.

Unique Features: Also known as Rambagh Fort, it is situated inside the Rambagh Palace and covers about 85 acres.

Barabar Caves

Location: Makhdumpur, Bihar, India

Significance: The oldest surviving rock-cut caves in India, dating back to the Maurya Empire.

Architecture: Carved entirely out of granite, featuring highly polished interiors and echo effects.

Historical Background: Built during the reign of Emperor Ashoka and his grandson Dasharatha Maurya, with inscriptions dedicated to the Ajivika sect.

Unique Features: Includes four main caves—Karan Chaupar, Lomas Rishi, Sudama, and Visvakarma. The Lomas Rishi Cave has an arch-like facade that influenced later Indian rock-cut architecture.

Bihar Museum

Location: Patna, Bihar, India

Established: August 15, 2015

Significance: A modern museum showcasing Bihar’s rich history, art, and culture.

Architecture: Designed by Maki and Associates, featuring contemporary structures spread over 5.6 hectares.

Historical Background: Built to house artifacts from ancient India up to 1764, while post-1764 artifacts remain in Patna Museum.

Unique Features: Includes galleries on archaeology, regional art, contemporary art, and a children's museum.

Patna Museum

Location: Patna, Bihar, India

Established: April 3, 1917

Significance: The oldest museum in Bihar, showcasing historical artifacts from ancient India.

Architecture: Built in Indo-Saracenic style, featuring domes, jharokha windows, and a central chhatri.

Historical Background: Originally housed in the Patna High Court before moving to its current location in 1929.

Unique Features: Displays relics of Gautama Buddha, the Didarganj Yakshi statue, and a fossilized tree estimated to be 200 million years old.

Veerayatan Museum

Location: Rajgir, Bihar, India

Significance: A Jain museum showcasing the history of the 24 Jain Tirthankaras.

Architecture: Features ornate 3D panel depictions made from wood and metal, resembling a dollhouse-like display.

Historical Background: Founded by Acharya Shri Chandanaji in 1973, focusing on Jain teachings and humanitarian service.

Unique Features: The museum houses an art gallery called "Shri Brahmi Kala Mandiram," displaying Jain philosophy through artistic representations.

Nalanda Museum

Location: Nalanda University Site Road, Silao, Bihar, India

Established: 1917

Significance: A prestigious site museum housing artifacts from the ancient Nalanda Mahavihara.

Architecture: Divided into four galleries, displaying sculptures, inscriptions, terracotta objects, and bronze artifacts.

Historical Background: Contains relics from the 5th to 12th centuries, showcasing Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain influences.

Unique Features: Exhibits include a scale model of the Nalanda ruins, ancient manuscripts, and a colossal Buddha statue.
